Majorana Chip - Pros and Cons

Pros Cons
Inherent error protection via topological qubits Experimental status; not yet proven to support stable, coherent qubits
Potentially reduces overhead in quantum error correction Hard to distinguish Majorana modes from other quasiparticles
More stable qubits design Lack of demonstrated quantum gates or logic operations
Can enable scalable, modular quantum architectures Cryogenic requirements since it needs extreme cooling close to absolute zero
Inspired by elegant topological physics Controversy over evidence since some scientists dispute the interpretations
Backed by Microsoft and academic collaborators Unclear timeline for practical implementation or commercialization
Could revolutionize quantum computing infrastructure Requires more peer-reviewed replication to confirm true topological behavior
