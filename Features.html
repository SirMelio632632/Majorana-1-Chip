Topological Qubits

Uses topological qubits based on Majorana zero modes, which are theorized to be more stable and fault-tolerant than conventional qubits.

Error Resilience

Encodes quantum information in a non-local way, offering natural resistance to decoherence and noise, reducing the need for complex error correction.

Nanowire-Superconductor Interface

Built using semiconductor nanowires coupled with superconducting materials (like aluminum), where Majorana particles may emerge at the ends.

Potential for Scalable Quantum Systems

If realized, these chips could simplify quantum hardware architecture, leading to scalable, modular quantum computers.

Hybrid Quantum Hardware

Combines materials science, quantum physics, and engineering — blending superconductivity, topological phases, and nano-fabrication.

Experimental Validation Focus

Designed primarily to test and validate the existence of Majorana zero modes, a foundational step toward building topological quantum computers.

Built for Cryogenic Operation

Operates at ultra-low temperatures, often close to absolute zero, to support superconducting and quantum behavior.

Designed by Microsoft Quantum (StationQ)

Part of Microsoft’s larger Azure Quantum initiative, working in collaboration with academic institutions like TU Delft and Purdue.
